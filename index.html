Yachting Guide & Tips

Before You Set Sail

Choose the Right Yacht:


Selecting the perfect yacht for your journey is crucial. Consider factors such as:


Number of Passengers: Ensure the yacht has enough cabins and space to comfortably accommodate everyone.

Destination: Different yachts may be better suited to various destinations. Consult with your yacht broker to match the yacht to your planned route.

Preferences and Amenities: Think about the onboard amenities and services you desire, such as water toys, dining options, entertainment systems or specific crew expertise.

Duration: If it's a long journey, comfort and facilities become even more vital.

Special Needs: If you have specific requirements such as accessibility, dietary needs or child-friendly features, make these known during the selection process.

VAT and Yacht Fuel Consumption

APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance): In most yacht charters, fuel is not included in the base price. Instead, clients pay an APA, which is usually around 20-30% of the charter fee. This is an estimate of the fuel and other costs (like food and port charges) that will be incurred during the charter.


Fuel Surcharge: Some charter companies may apply a fuel surcharge if fuel prices increase dramatically before the charter period.


Actuals: After the charter, the actual fuel consumption is calculated. If it's less than the APA, the difference is refunded to the client. If it's more, an additional payment is required.
