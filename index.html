FAQs

This section addresses common queries, offering expert advice on everything from the cost of boat hire in Ibiza to the best times to visit. Whether you're curious about the specific amenities onboard, safety protocols, or how to personalise your sailing itinerary, our FAQs are designed to provide you with the clarity and confidence to book your yacht in Ibiza


Are Ibiza boat parties worth it?

Yes, Ibiza boat rental parties are renowned for their vibrant atmosphere, combining scenic beauty with world-class DJs and entertainment. They offer a unique way to experience the island's famous nightlife on the water.

How much does it cost a boat hire in Ibiza town?

The cost varies widely depending on the yacht's size, level of luxury, and charters duration. Day charters can start from a few thousand euros, while a week-long hire on a superyacht may cost tens of thousands.

What's the best time to experience Ibiza by yacht?

For the liveliest atmosphere, July and August are peak season. If you prefer a quieter pace, May, June, and summer, September, and October are lovely, with the added bonus of fewer crowds.

What are the must-visit spots on a yacht in Ibiza and Formentera?

Highlights include the crystal-clear waters of Cala Comte, the secluded bays of Cala Salada, breathtaking sunsets at Es Vedrà, and the pristine sands of Ses Illetes in Formentera

What is included in a yacht charter fee?

Our rent charters fee typically includes the rental of the yacht or boat itself, the attendance of a professional staff, and all necessary safety equipment. Standard provisions may cover basic amenities, but additional costs such as fuel, marina fees, food and beverage expenses, and any special requests (e.g., water sports equipment, guided tours) are usually not included and can be arranged for an extra fee. It's important to discuss these details upfront to tailor the charter to your preferences while managing budget expectations.

What is the rental Cancellation Policy for a boat in Ibiza?

More than 90 days before the charter start date: Clients may cancel their booking and receive a full refund of any amounts paid, less an administrative fee to cover processing costs.


60 to 89 days before the charter start date: Clients are eligible to receive a 50% refund of the total charter fee.


Less than 60 days before the charter start date: No refunds will be available, as we are unlikely to rebook the yacht for the specified period on short notice.


We strongly recommend clients consider travel insurance that covers charter cancellations due to unforeseen circumstances such as illness or travel disruptions. This ensures that your investment is protected, and you can look forward to your luxury yacht charter experience with peace of mind.

How Many Marinas are on the Island of Ibiza, and what are their features?

Spain Marina Ibiza: Considered one of the most prestigious in Balearic islands, Marina Ibiza is not only a haven for luxury yachts and boat in Ibiza but also a lively spot filled with upscale restaurants, shops, and nightlife options.


Spain Marina Botafoch: Located just across from Ibiza Town, Marina Botafoch is known for its exclusive services and facilities, including 428 moorings for yachts up to 30 meters.


Spain Santa Eulària des Riu Marina: Situated on the east coast of the island, the Santa Eulalia Marina is a family-friendly option with 755 moorings for boats up to 22 meters.


Spain Club Náutico Ibiza: Located in Ibiza Town, this marina is one of the oldest on the island, known for its sailing school and community sail-focused locals events.


Spain Port de Sant Antoni: On the west coast of Ibiza, this marina is part of the town of Sant Antoni de Portmany and offers a more laid-back vibe. It accommodates charter boats with over 245 moorings and is a popular starting point for sunset cruises.


Spain Marina Magna: Situated in the heart of Ibiza Town, ibiza Marina Magna is a smaller marina, offering a boutique experience with around 85 moorings.
